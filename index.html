Undergraduate Thesis

Research at the intersection of mathematics, computation, and history (in the true spirit of the liberal arts).

Technical Writing

A Historical Approach to Markov Chain Monte Carlo

This work traces the history of the Metropolis-Hastings (MH) algorithm, a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) method still widely used today. Simulations (Python, NumPy, Google Colab) modeled after the findings of its original creators, Metropolis et al. (1953) and Hastings (1970), accompany this work. At its culmination, I delivered a 20-minute talk to departmental faculty and students.

Research Area Probability Methods Historical analysis, computational simulation, and algorithmic analysis. Outcome Work is underway for an adaptation of my thesis to be submitted to the Transforming Instruction in Undergraduate Mathematics via Primary Historical Sources (TRIUMPHS) Project.
