A Historical Approach to Markov Chain Monte Carlo

This work traces the history of the Metropolis-Hastings (MH) algorithm, a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) method still widely used today. I built simulations (Python, NumPy, Google Colab) modeled after the findings of its original creators, Metropolis et al. (1953) and Hastings (1970), accompany this work. At its culmination, I delivered a 20-minute talk to departmental faculty and students.

Research Area Probability Methods Historical analysis, computational simulation, and algorithmic analysis. Outcome I am currently adapting my thesis to be published under the Transforming Instruction in Undergraduate Mathematics via Primary Historical Sources (TRIUMPHS) Project.
